# Platosd / Tavern Bash: Commercial Readiness Audit

Date: 2026-07-17. Build audited: 0.99.0 (The Signature Wares), committed by a concurrent session mid-audit. Method: full first-hand read of engine.js and data.js, six parallel subsystem analyses, two live balance sims (Quick and Long), a 490-test suite run (all green), and the design docs and reflection notes.

Punctuation note: this file is written dash-free so it can live in the repo without tripping the dash scan.

---

## Part 0: The seven root causes

Nearly every one of the 100 findings below traces to one of these seven underlying weaknesses. Fix the roots and dozens of symptoms resolve together.

**R1. Content breadth is being built at the expense of experienced depth.** The catalog is genuinely large (82 wares, 69 monster boards, 16 Omens, 8 heroes, a 10-level Lantern, Aspects, Affixes, Chorus, Signatures). But the played experience is a solved economy feeding a fast-weapon board across three trivial districts into one wall. The last six versions (0.94 to 0.99) were all content systems (Almanac, Aspects, Affixes, Chorus, Omens, Signatures), while the designer's own ROADMAP said the real gaps are onboarding, difficulty shape, return motivation, and mobile delivery. The roadmap even warns "adding another district extends repetition rather than replayability" and "build the smaller systems that create the same player outcomes." Effort is going into content the player cannot see or feel, not into the fundamentals that make content matter.

**R2. All difficulty lives at the Dragon Gate.** Live sim, current build: Quick districts 1 to 3 die 0.0 / 0.0 / 0.4 percent and win 92.5 / 89.6 / 86.4 percent first attempt; district 4 dies 22.8 percent and wins 51.6 percent. Long is the same shape (D4 deaths 27.5 percent). Players never drop below 30 of 40 Resolve until the Gate. Three quarters of every run is a tensionless victory lap, and all failure and frustration is concentrated on one boss that carries an anti-player snowball.

**R3. The economy is solved, so the 50 percent of playtime spent in the market is procedure, not decision.** Reroll 1 gold plus free freeze plus a shallow TIERCOST (41 gold buys all six tiers) plus no gold sink means every competent run converges to tier 5, all Gold. Diamond, the stated "real late-game goal," is reached 0.0 to 0.3 percent of the time. There is no economy or greed archetype (purse and ledger are filtered out of the shop).

**R4. Combat is a non-interactive spectator event whose payoff is under-delivered and unverified.** All agency is pre-fight; the fight is watch-only. It is 19 percent of playtime, its feedback layer (cooldown rings, duel portraits) is faint and shrinks in the primary landscape orientation, and the entire visual layer has never been verified because the dev preview throttles rAF.

**R5. The depth that exists is invisible, unreachable, or illegible.** 16 signature wares are invisible in the Almanac and a run sees only 2. 18 treasure uniques (the deepest hook content) are gated to the last district, so they are rewards for winning rather than tools to win. Hook wares carry dense conditional prose on tiny cards. Featured tags and run modifiers are a tap deep. The game is far deeper than it feels.

**R6. Onboarding is absent.** The Tutorial button starts an ordinary run; the guided coach system is orphaned dead code; the in-game help still describes the deleted 8-merchant lobby. A first-timer meets two health layers, fusion, doors, storm, the route map, Lantern, and unlocks with zero teaching. This is the single largest barrier to a first session converting and the top negative-review risk.

**R7. The project has outrun its own source of truth, and the shared single checkout is actively drifting.** ROADMAP says build 0.87 while the repo is 0.99; version numbers have decoupled from milestones; CLAUDE.md and AGENTS.md contradict each other on deploy target and hero weighting; a concurrent agent committed a feature during this read-only audit. MAP_VERSION fragility retires every in-progress run on any content add. Reward and metrics logic is smeared across many confusingly named modules.

## Part 1: The 100 highest-impact improvements, ranked

Fields per item: Why it matters, Root cause, Player impact, Effort (L/M/H), Risk, Dependencies, Approach.

### Tier A: launch-gating fundamentals (1 to 20)

**1. Build a real route-native tutorial.**
Why: onboarding is the top conversion barrier and the top review risk. Root: R6. The coach system (G.tut, .coach cards, .tuthl highlights) is orphaned; #inTut calls newRoute('quick') with zero guidance; help text describes the deleted lobby. Player impact: enormous for first-session retention. Effort: M. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: a fixed-seed short scripted route with 6 to 8 skippable contextual callouts teaching hero identity, buying, gold burn, fusion (3 then 2 then 2), board slots, scouting a door, Resolve, the storm, and one event. Record completion and abandonment. Repurpose the surviving .coach CSS.

**2. Reshape the difficulty curve so districts 1 to 3 carry real jeopardy.**
Why: 75 percent of every run has zero tension (R2). Root: near-flat threat and HP scaling then a spike at the Gate. Player impact: transforms the run from victory-lap-then-wall into a rising arc. Effort: M. Risk: Med (balance). Deps: item 7 (validated sim). Approach: redistribute risk without changing total length. Raise D2 and D3 first-attempt loss to the 65 to 80 percent band; ensure first-attempt win falls monotonically by district with no adjacent drop over 15 points; soften the Gate cliff so Long clear lands in the roadmap's own 55 to 70 percent target (it is currently 71.3, too easy).

**3. Un-solve the economy.**
Why: the market is 50.8 percent of playtime and converges to one procedure (R3). Root: cheap reroll, free freeze, shallow tier cost, no sink, Diamond unreachable. Player impact: turns shopping into strategy. Effort: M. Risk: Med. Deps: 7, 19. Approach: pick two or three levers, not all: scale reroll cost within a market, make freeze cost or limit it, deepen TIERCOST, and add a genuine gold sink (a bankable or spendable meta use) so "spend now vs save" is a real tension. Validate each with the sim before shipping.

**4. Viewport-cap every overlay with a pinned action footer.**
Why: the loss end-screen, hero pick, mode pick, and reveal overflow at 390px and push the primary action below the fold (R4). Root: overlays sized for tall viewports. Player impact: removes a mobile blocker that hits every run's end. Effort: L. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: give .ov card bodies max-height calc(100dvh minus footer) with an internal scroll region and a fixed footer holding New Run / Copy / Continue. Shrink the 124px skull icon.

**5. Surface build direction on the draft screen.**
Why: featured tags, Lantern rules, storm shift, and the district Affix are all one tap deep behind #chipAno; a new player cannot see what to build toward when it matters (R5). Root: acts pass folded context into a drawer to save space. Player impact: makes every market a directed choice. Effort: L. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: a persistent one-line "featured tonight" strip on the market stage and a small Affix glyph on the scout card.

**6. Move the deepest content earlier in the run.**
Why: the 18 R8 treasure uniques (the richest hooks) are gated to D4 via TREASURE_GATE {1:1,2:2,3:4,4:6}, so they are acquired when the run is essentially over (R5). Root: treasure gating tuned as end-reward. Player impact: gives players tools to build with, not trophies. Effort: M. Risk: Med (balance and fusion dilution). Deps: 7. Approach: seed a small number of tier-appropriate treasure hooks from D2 onward; keep the marquee uniques late but let build-defining hooks arrive mid-run.

**7. Validate heroes and treasure uniques in the balance sim.**
Why: route-sim.js omits HEROES[].mod and abstracts all 18 unique hooks, so 8 heroes (including combat-rewriting venom and architect) and 18 uniques ship on feel (R1, R7). Root: sim scope. Player impact: prevents hidden outliers reaching launch. Effort: M. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: extend the harness to apply hero mods and to construct treasure-unique boards, then run the hero-by-Omen matrix the roadmap already specced (0.94). Label each mechanic full / proxy / blind in a coverage manifest.

**8. On-device combat feedback pass.**
Why: the fight is the payoff and its feedback is faint and unverified (R4). Root: dev preview throttles rAF; the active .cdf ring is a thin low-opacity band; the dead .ring is display:none yet still written each frame; duel portraits shrink to 30px in landscape. Player impact: makes wins and losses legible and satisfying. Effort: M. Risk: Low. Deps: on-device session. Approach: delete the dead ring writes, raise .cdf contrast, restore duel-portrait size in landscape, and verify on an actual iPhone (the preview cannot).

**9. Raise every core-loop control to a 44 by 44 hit area.**
Why: .spdbtn (34x24 mid-fight), .btn.mini (30px), .lstep (34px), and in-run .mute (30px) violate the 44px floor and the no-hover mandate. Root: density passes traded ergonomics for fit. Player impact: fewer misfires, less frustration. Effort: L. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: enforce min 44px on the listed controls; extend the automated touch-target check beyond route nodes to market, Vault, sound, and combat-speed.

**10. Rewrite the in-game help and make a rules reference reachable in-run.**
Why: the only text help describes the retired lobby ("Eight merchants, 40 health each") and is hidden in body.run (R6). Root: help never updated after the campaign replaced the lobby. Player impact: gives lost players an anchor. Effort: L. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: rewrite .help for route rules; add a How to Play entry reachable from the title and during a run.

**11. Make hook-ware behavior legible on the card.**
Why: effChips renders only base fx; multi-clause hook rules live entirely in prose unreadable at a glance on 844x390 (R5). Root: mechanical depth outran the card surface. Player impact: lets players actually use the deep content. Effort: M. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: render a compact icon grammar for the common hook shapes (threshold, on-kill, on-cleanse, convert), keep prose as a tap-to-expand, and cap the at-a-glance line.

**12. Ship the player history and mastery screen.**
Why: the roadmap's named first retention layer (0.92) is still owed; the data already exists (R1). Root: content sprint jumped ahead of it. Player impact: gives every run a lasting result and a reason to return. Effort: M. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: turn the local report archive player-facing: recent runs with result / hero / Omen / duration / seed / final build, personal bests per hero and route, discovery pages, and seed replay. Local, account-free.

**13. Personalize the unlock hint and generalize the sealed-reward line.**
Why: nextUnlockHint walks TRIGGERS in fixed order (tells a player one night from a feat to chase a different one), and nextSealedHint computes proximity over only 4 of about 31 triggers (R5). Root: static ordering as a stand-in for nearest-target. Player impact: keeps the "earn the next thing" pull honest. Effort: M. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: compute per-player proximity across all triggers, surface the nearest, and generalize the end-screen sealed line to the full trigger set including the 0.98 guild Omens.

**14. Redesign the Azhdaha anti-player snowball.**
Why: hasteMates:0.5 permanently speeds surviving heads when you kill one, so progress accelerates your own death; it reads as unfair, not hard (R2). Root: a punish-progress mechanic on a gate boss. Player impact: removes the run's most rage-inducing moment. Effort: L. Risk: Med (it is a signature fight). Deps: 2, 7. Approach: invert or cap it (killing a head weakens the survivors, or the haste decays), or convert it to a telegraphed enrage the player can race.

**15. Rebalance bulwark so one cheap tank does not wall all weapons.**
Why: pickTarget returns the first alive bulwark unconditionally before the mode check, so a single cd:0 integMul:2.2 Brass Buckler reroutes every non-flying weapon to one slot (R4). Root: unconditional taunt plus cheap high-integrity bulwark. Player impact: reopens weapon play and defensive counterplay. Effort: M. Risk: Med (touches targeting and golden traces). Deps: approved engine-behavior change. Approach: cap bulwark integrity or let overflow and maxinteg partially bypass; retune the shield category around it.

**16. Decouple map generation from the full ITEMS pool to stop retiring runs on content add.**
Why: treasureWareIds and genMap read the whole ITEMS pool, so adding any non-unique ware shifts rollTreasure and forces a MAP_VERSION bump that drops every in-progress save (R7). The game is in a content-add phase, so this fires constantly. Root: map stores no nodes and derives treasure from the live pool. Player impact: stops punishing active players on every update. Effort: M. Risk: Med. Deps: none. Approach: freeze the treasure pool by a content-epoch id stamped into the run, or store resolved treasure ids in the save so new content does not shift existing runs.

**17. Reconcile the canonical state and retire the doc contradictions.**
Why: ROADMAP says 0.87, repo is 0.99; CLAUDE.md and AGENTS.md disagree on Netlify vs Pages and on the stale x2.2 hero weight; version numbers have decoupled from milestones (R7). Root: no single current-state authority. Player impact: indirect, prevents build-on-wrong-assumption defects. Effort: L. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: one short tracked state file (version, deploy target, save and map schema versions, active roadmap slot); move history out of CLAUDE.md; make AGENTS.md a pointer plus deltas.

**18. Enforce worktree or branch isolation for concurrent agents.**
Why: a feature commit landed in the shared checkout during this read-only audit; the reflection notes rank this the number one process cost (R7). Root: single shared working tree, multiple writers. Player impact: indirect, prevents cross-agent contamination and lost work. Effort: L. Risk: Low. Deps: none. Approach: one worktree or branch per active implementation agent, a version reservation in the state file, single-writer commit rule.

**19. Resolve Diamond: make it reachable or cut the tier.**
Why: RSTAT tops at 7 (Diamond) but it is reached 0.0 to 0.3 percent, so the whole top of the power curve is dead content and fusion has no felt apex (R3). Root: 7 bronze copies is unreachable in a normal-length run. Player impact: gives fusion a climax or removes a false promise. Effort: L. Risk: Med. Deps: 3. Approach: either lower the Diamond copy cost or add a rare Diamond-completion path (a Gate Camp forge, an Omen), or cut Diamond and re-slope Gold as the apex.

**20. Make door risk and Slip Past meaningful before D4.**
Why: with 90 percent win and 0 percent death in D1 to D3, Slip Past is never worth its Resolve and the whole risk apparatus is inert until too late (R2). Root: no early jeopardy for risk to weigh against. Player impact: turns routing into a live decision. Effort: M. Risk: Med. Deps: 2. Approach: as difficulty ramps (item 2), Slip Past and gilded doors become genuine push-your-luck choices; tie a small reward to challenging over slipping.

## Part 3: The 10 biggest hidden risks to the project

1. **Concurrent single-checkout drift.** A feature commit landed during a read-only audit; this can silently contaminate work and lose changes. Highest process risk.
2. **MAP_VERSION fragility.** Every content add retires in-progress runs; the game is in a content-add phase, so this quietly punishes players on nearly every update.
3. **Unmeasured heroes and treasure uniques.** venom and architect rewrite combat rules and nothing validates them; a launch outlier likely lives here.
4. **Gilded-column balance is under-tested.** The design's own harness found three Aspect band breaches under gild that the plain-biased sim missed; gilded Souk Rats already sits at a 40 percent cliff.
5. **On-device fight rendering is unverified.** The whole visual and feel layer has never been confirmed on hardware because the preview throttles rAF.
6. **The signature system shipped without live-harness or review validation.** Its own doc names the fixture pass as "the first build step," not done.
7. **The retention loop terminates early.** The novelty chase ends in 10 to 15 runs and the endgame is pure grind; churn will spike right after players stop unlocking new things.
8. **Canonical-state decoupling.** Version numbers no longer map to milestones; a future change built on the wrong assumption is likely.
9. **Reward exactly-once logic is smeared across four modules.** The safety-critical path with the highest bug surface is the least consolidated.
10. **Mobile delivery is incomplete.** Lazy precache, external fonts, undecided name, no native wrap: the last mile to a store-ready installed app is unbuilt.

2. **The market economy.** Replace the solved reroll-freeze-tier procedure with a real spend-vs-save tension and a reachable fusion apex.

## Part 9: Honest answers

**If Platosd launched today, what Steam review score would you expect and why?**
Mixed to Mostly Positive at best, realistically Mixed (about 65 to 72 percent). The art, theme, and engine would earn genuine praise, but three things drag it down hard: no tutorial (a large share of first sessions bounce confused), a difficulty curve that is trivial for 75 percent of a run then a frustrating wall, and a solved, repetitive economy. Mobile friction and illegible depth compound it. The ceiling is higher than the score suggests, because the foundations are strong; the floor is set by the missing fundamentals the designer's own roadmap already named.

**The three biggest reasons players would stop playing.**
1. They never understood the game (no onboarding) and quit in the first session.
2. They hit the Dragon Gate wall after a tensionless run and it feels unfair, not hard.
3. They solved the economy and the runs started feeling identical, and the novelty chase ran out in 10 to 15 runs with only grind left.

**The three biggest reasons players would recommend it.**
1. It looks and sounds genuinely premium and distinctive (the night-market identity).
2. The heroes and the fusion-plus-hooks depth create real build variety once you see it.
3. It runs great and respects your time (deterministic, stable, quick runs, honest previews).

**What feature or mechanic is currently holding the game back the most?**
The difficulty curve. Everything the game does well (build variety, scouting, risk-reward, the economy) is neutralized by three districts of zero jeopardy into one wall. Fix the ramp and the entire existing kit suddenly gets to matter across the whole run instead of only at the end. Onboarding is the close second, but onboarding teaches a game whose core arc is currently flat.

**If you had only one month before launch, what would you focus on?**
The Tier A fundamentals, in this order: (1) a real tutorial, (2) reshape the difficulty curve so D1 to D3 have real jeopardy and the Gate is the peak of a ramp, (3) un-solve the economy with two or three levers, (4) fix the mobile overlay overflow and 44px controls, (5) validate heroes and treasure uniques in the sim, (6) ship the history screen, (7) verify the fight on device. Ship no new content. Every one of these is what the designer's own roadmap and reflection already prioritized and the content sprint skipped.

**If you had one year before launch, what would you redesign from the ground up?**
The whole progression-and-pacing spine as one integrated arc: a monotonic difficulty ramp, an economy with real spend-vs-save tension and a reachable fusion apex, treasure and depth delivered mid-run as tools, a Long second act that is mechanically new, and a retention loop that paces novelty across the mid-game and surfaces the Lantern climb. In parallel, consolidate the route architecture (one reward transaction API, one meta profile, a content-epoch save model) and add one moment of in-fight agency to raise the combat ceiling for skilled and streaming players.

**What single change would produce the greatest increase in player satisfaction?**
Reshape the difficulty curve so the run is a rising arc instead of a flat plain and a wall. It makes the existing systems matter, removes the game's most frustrating moment, and creates tension in the 75 percent of the run that is currently empty.

**What single change would produce the greatest increase in long-term retention?**
Build the player history and mastery screen and pace real novelty into the mid-game so the "earn the next thing" loop does not die at run 15. Retention lives on a reason to return and a sense of accumulating mastery; today both evaporate once the early unlock firehose ends.
